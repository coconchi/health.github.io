Healthcare Model

Analysis for complex systems

Health systems are highly complex, and are formed by the interactions of many agents, including patients, providers, insurance companies or organizations, and more. Many countries around the world are facing tough challenges in diverse systems: rising costs, insufficient insurance coverage, and poor public health are just some of the difficulties that policymakers are attempting to address. The Simulation Unit hs created an agent-based model of a national health system with the objective of informing health policy through ex-ante analysis of policy changes.

The model includes patients, providers, insurance companies, and exogenous health shocks, and is informative about several outcomes, including health status of the population, health care costs, insurance coverage, and quality of care. Because the model is a bottom-up, agent-based model instead of a standard general equilibrium model, it recognizes the complexity inherent in these systems, and is able to incorporate knowledge of diverse fields such as behavioral economics and epidemiology.

Flexible and context-specific

The model is programmed for maximum flexibility, and its inputs can be adapted to a wide variety of countries and health contexts. This is acheived through adapting the insurance landscape to imitate the national mix of public and private insurance as well as regulation. The model also equipped to receive epidemiological data and information about health expenditures and economic variables, such as income inequality.
